Born a Crime – Individual Reflection

By Lwam Bisrat

" Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage."

This quote irritated me when it comes to gender roles. Abel, in theory, desires a woman who conforms to traditional

expectations, someone subservient and obedient. However, his heart betrays him; he's drawn to women with independent

spirits, women who refuse to be confined. The tragic irony is that Abel, driven by his own insecurities or desire for

control, ultimately seeks to "cage" the very freedom he admires.

Many men claim to want an "independent " woman-educated,ambitions,outspoken but still expect her to shrink herself in

private.for example a boyfriend praises his girlfriends career success but sulks when she works late but tradition

doesn't mean oppression.Like patricia,breaking free from these cages isn't just rebellion;it's survival.

This quote can resonate with girls and women for several reasons:

-Recognition of a Familiar Pattern: Many women have encountered men, or know of men, who express a desire for a "traditional" partner while simultaneously being drawn to women who are independent and strong.

- Fear of Being "Caged": The phrase "put her in a cage" evokes a strong emotional response. It speaks to the fear of losing one's autonomy, freedom, and sense of self within a relationship. Women who value their independence might find this prospect particularly alarming, as it represents a potential loss of control over their own lives.

-Warning Sign Recognition: The quote can serve as a warning sign, alerting women to be wary of partners who express a desire for a "traditional" wife while exhibiting controlling tendencies. It encourages women to be vigilant and protect their independence within relationships

-Awareness of Double Standards: The quote exposes a double standard: the expectation that women should be both independent and subservient. It highlights the pressure women face to conform to conflicting ideals, often leading to a sense of being "damned if you do, damned if you don't.
