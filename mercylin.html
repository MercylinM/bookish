Born a Crime – Individual Reflection

By Mercylin Muthoni

“Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'”

This quote made me pause. It speaks to a kind of love that isn’t really love at all — the kind that admires someone’s freedom, only to later try to take it away. I’ve seen this pattern in the world around me: people drawn to strong personalities, but uncomfortable when that strength shows up in ways they can’t control. Abel’s desire for a “traditional” wife was really about wanting dominance, not connection. And Patricia, who refused to be small, paid the price for being free.

What struck me is how relevant this still is. Even today, people praise “independent women” but expect them to shrink once they’re in a relationship. Being soft-spoken is expected, being assertive is often labeled as aggressive. There’s still a quiet pressure to make yourself more palatable. That’s why Patricia stands out, she refused to change who she was to make Abel feel more powerful.

Her strength wasn’t loud or performative, it was steady. She raised Trevor to think for himself, to see beyond systems designed to make him feel less. And that made her dangerous in the eyes of a man who needed control to feel whole.

This quote isn’t just about marriage, it’s about the power struggles that live inside so many relationships. True love doesn’t fear freedom; it makes room for it. Patricia understood that. Abel never did.
