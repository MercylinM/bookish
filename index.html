BORN A CRIME

Reviewed by Team Bookish

Born a crime is a powerful memoir by Trevor Noah which has profoundly used humor to tell a story that relates to many people in African settings. He humorously explores the complexities of his identity as a mixed-race child navigating a divided society. He dissects issues such as race,resilience and the profound influence of his mother. This memoir serves as a reflection on the broader human experience offering insights into the struggles living in a fractured world.

DEAR IJEAWELE

Reviewed by Team Bookish

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie shares fifteen crucial suggestions for young girls to help them navigate a world that imposes limitations on gender roles. In her book, she shares the importance of building confidence,independence and resilience in girls.She shares a letter to her friend as a guide and a manifesto as she advocates for gender equality. She uses her insightful words to challenge the norm where society has set expectations for women.
