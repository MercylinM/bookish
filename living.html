Born a Crime - Individual Reflection

By Habtamnesh Marye

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife, but he never fell in love with subservient women... he only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage"

It really struck me because it perfectly captures the tension that exists in many relationships, not just in the context of apartheid but in today’s world as well. It made me think about how some men, even while valuing a woman’s independence, still want to control her in some way, limiting her freedom under the guise of love or tradition.

When I read this, I couldn't help but think about how it resonates with the contradictions in gender roles today. On one hand, we live in a world that increasingly supports women's independence. Women are going after their careers, speaking out, and gaining more power in relationships. But then, on the other hand, there are still many expectations placed on women to behave in certain ways, especially in relationships. We’re expected to be strong and independent, but the moment we challenge traditional roles or assert our freedom, it seems like there's always an underlying expectation for us to "return to our place."

In my view, this is a dynamic I see in many relationships today, including those in my community. For example, women can achieve so much in their careers, but there is still pressure to maintain traditional roles at home. Many women juggle professional responsibilities but are expected to be the primary caregivers, to manage the household, and fulfill certain expectations about what it means to be a “good wife.” This is very much like Abel’s idea of putting a woman in a “cage.” It's as if society allows us to be "free," but only in ways that don't challenge the dominant power structures.
