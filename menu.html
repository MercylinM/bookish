Feeling like an outsider


Trevor describes feeling like an outsider, as if he was "all by myself"
even while being surrounded by others. He felt the need to be anonymous
and invisible rather than seeking acceptance for his whole self.
Trevor Noah frequently describes feeling invisible at school,
particularly ,at Sandringham,a high-school with students of all
races and backgrounds.Trevor found it difficult to find a "niche".
He felt like he wasn't quite part of the black community or the white
community, leading to a sense of isolation and invisibility.



people in his school were grouped by color in most cases.
even with so many different groups to choose from,he was not a .
natural constituent of any particular one .The colored kids would shit .
on Trevor all the time for being too black .even if he was skilled with the
white kids not to get bullied by them , they were always going shooping and movies
and he didnt have money so he was out of the mix there,too. He learned to navigate
these spaces by becoming a "chameleon,"he learned how to blend,he learned that even though he didnot.
belong to one group he could be a part of any group,but this constant performance also.
contributed to his feeling of being unseen.
