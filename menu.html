Humor as Armor

Trevor challenges the nuns and priests about taking the holy communion
despite being a christian. He argues that Jesus would not be discriminated
despite him being a jew.He challenges them if they would restrict him from
taking holy communion if he walked in and asked for it.Trevor uses his wit
and ability to use humor to address serious issues like hypocrisy and
inequality within institutions.It also reflects his critical thinking and
willingness to challenge authority even as a child.


Trevor reminisces how he unknowingly insults a taxi driver in zulu.Trevor
uses a phrase that the driver terms it as disrespectful.The driver bocomes
furious and throws Trevor out of a moving taxi.Instead of focusing on the
danger,Trevor uses humor to describe the absurdity of the situation and
his naivety,turning a frightening experience into a humorous story.


Trevor highlights his famil's deep religious beliefs and the intersection
of religion with colonial influence.One memorable example is when he
humorously notes that his family believed that God paid more attention
to prayers in English.This belief is deeply rooted in the colonial legacy of
English as a dominant language,reflects how colonial influence shaped
their their spiritual practices.He also describes how sundays were purely
dedicated to going to church and how grueling the experience was to
him.However, due to his mother's unwavering faith and belief in power
of prayer he was constantly reminded that he needed to pray and go
to church.


Trevor describes his experience of being thrown out of a moving minibus
by the taxi driver.Apparently, the driver was angry that Trevor had insulted
him unknowingly in zulu ,a language he was still learning.Instead of dwelling
on the danger and humiliation of the incident ,Trevor recounts it with humor
,highlighting the absurdity of the situation and his own naivety.This shows
how Trevor uses humor to process and rise above difficult moments.

Legacy of Apartheid

The government built Bantu schools which were meant to teach metrics
and agriculture which is how to count potatoes,how to pave roads,chop
wood and fill the soil.They claim that it does not serve the Bantu to learn
history and science because he is primitive and it will only mislead him,
showing him pastures in which he is not allowed to graze.


In addition,the Chinese were considered black by the goverment since
they weren't enough Chinese people to warrant devising a whole separate
classification.The Japanese on the other hand were considered white
since the government wanted to establish good relations in order to
import their fancy cars and electronics.


Systems and structures often perpetuate systemic inequality to execute
power and many times it is backed up by individual biases.For instance,
the government during apartheid built Bantu schools which were meant
to teach metrics and agriculture. Systemic inequality prevents a group of
people from exercising their freedom thereby constricting them to rules
and laws that are inhuman in nature.


Some of the examples of systemic inequality include:

  • Over-policing and harsh sentencing for marginalized communities
    leading to disproportionate icarceration rates.
  • Underfunded schools in disadvantaged areas lack resources and
    access to high quality education and achievement gaps based on
    race and socioeconomic status.
  • Communities of color and low income communities are disproportionately
    exposed to environmental hazards like pollution and lack access to green
    spaces and resources.
